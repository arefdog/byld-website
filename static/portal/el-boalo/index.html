The site and the binding planning

The plot is Sector 1B of the El Boalo NNSS. The official ordenación ficha, published in the BOCM, gives the binding parameters. It is developable residential land, obtained by agreement with the owner, not protected rústico.

The parameters describe an exclusive, low-density villa estate: eleven homes per hectare, two storeys, an average home near 226 m², and half the land ceded as public roads and green space, run by a private conservation entity. The planning itself prescribes the premium, landscape-led product, and the low delivered density is the strongest answer to local opposition to urban expansion.

03

Masterplan and recommended use

The BYLD masterplan tool lays the programme along the site's own lines: circulation first, buildings on the contours, the regulated housing by the town, and most of the land kept open. Because the planning caps units, value comes from price per home, not more homes.

ZoneProgramme
Hotel lineSmall operator-light wellness hotel and spa (~20–30 keys) along the access contour, screened from the open horizon
VillasFree-market and premium residences stepped on the S/SE contours for sun and views

Equestrian & farmLight timber structures and a kitchen garden on the east meadow
Dehesa commonsThe majority of the land held open: trails on the dry-stone-wall spines, dark-sky, no fences

Regulatory frame (Ley 9/2001, from the masterplan cessions ledger): edificability 0.25 = 17,621 m² across 78 homes; ~50% of the land ceded as roads, green and equipment; VPP ≥ 30% of residential edificability; green ≥ 15 m²/100 m² built; parking ≥ 1.5 spaces/100 m²; 10% aprovechamiento to the ayuntamiento.

Residential is the engine. Up to 78 low-density homes, VPP held at the floor as a compact block by the village edge, free-market and premium homes as villas on the contours.

The wellness hotel is the amenity, not the anchor. Small and operator-light (20 to 30 keys): built larger it dilutes the return, since a five-star costs ~€3,900/m² to build yet is worth far less per m² as an asset than a villa is to sell. Underwrite it on the premium it creates and its operating yield, never on build-to-sell.

The setting is bought cheaply. Adjacent rústico holds the grounds, trails and equestrian centre and protects the views, moving the open-space cost off expensive sector land.

05

The peer category: serviced retreats in Europe

El Boalo is not competing with the local corridor; it is entering the category of Europe's serviced, branded destination retreats. Set against them on the terms that drive value, price, proximity to a wealthy city, climate and brand, its position is distinctive.

Price: entry tier, with headroom. The category runs from ~€4,375/m² (Ombria) and ~€5,760/m² (Costa Navarino) up to ~€10–15k (Reschio) and ~€16–18k (Andermatt). El Boalo's €4,500–5,500/m² sits at the entry, alongside the branded Algarve and Greek resorts, with clear headroom toward the premium tier as a brand and track record build.

Proximity: El Boalo's singular advantage. It is 45 minutes from Madrid, a metro of 7.1 million and one of Europe's wealthiest. No peer is that close to a capital of that scale: Costa Navarino is ~3.5 hours from Athens, Reschio ~3 from Rome, Andermatt 90 minutes from Zurich, Ombria near a regional airport town. Proximity to a large, affluent, year-round catchment is the rarest attribute a retreat can hold, and it is what underwrites the one-night-escape demand the others cannot.

Climate: four seasons, year-round demand. At 920 m, El Boalo offers a cool-air escape from Madrid's fierce summers (the city routinely tops 35°C), snow within reach in winter, and temperate spring and autumn. Its Mediterranean peers skew to a summer season; Andermatt is winter-ski-led; only Reschio shares the four-season profile. With proximity, El Boalo is the least seasonally dependent of the set.

Brand: the unrealised premium. Branded residences command roughly +32% in resort markets (Savills / Knight Frank). Three peers carry a hotel brand (Viceroy, Mandarin Oriental, Elie Saab) and Reschio a strong independent one. El Boalo has none yet, which is both the opportunity (the premium is available) and the task: securing a wellness or hospitality operator would capture the uplift and de-risk absorption.

Roughly €8 to 9M, about 15% on GDV and ~22% on cost, a normal developer margin, before any hotel value. VPP is delivered near cost as an obligation; the free-market and premium villas carry the profit. Every €250/m² achieved on the ~12,300 m² of open-market product is about €3M of profit, so the scheme rewards premium execution and branding. The €4M land is priced full against the local Sector 10C land value (~€250/m² buildable, urbanised), so buy it as a vertical developer capturing the building margin, stage payments to Plan Parcial milestones, and pre-sell off-plan.

07

What to verify next

  • PGOU redraft and local opposition.

    The 1996 NNSS is being replaced; confirm Sector 1B survives the new plan. The low-density, half-green scheme is the best political defence.

  • Vía pecuaria deslinde.

    The ficha requires authorisation from Vías Pecuarias and Comisaría de Aguas; secure the deslinde early, as it shapes the developable area and the mandated Ronda Este connection.

  • Cession and urbanization load.

    Only 50% of the land is privately appropriable, plus a 10% aprovechamiento cession and full urbanization within 8 years. Model it fully before pricing the land.

  • Plan Parcial unit mix.

    Resolve the 30% VPP against the 78-home cap and large average home size, with a compact VPP block and large free-market villas.
