What this shows

A physically accurate sun for the El Boalo / Guadarrama site (40.7°N). The round roof casts a real shadow onto the deck and the central glass core. Drag Day and Time, or use Sweep day, and orbit the model.

  • The overhang is a powerful, all-round sunshade. Oversailing the core by ~2.75 m, it keeps the glass in shade whenever the sun is above ~8° — i.e. essentially the whole day, every season.
  • Only low sunrise/sunset light reaches under the eaves. Set Time to ~6:00 or ~19:00 to see the rays rake across the deck and into the core.
  • Open question for Madrid: the roof shades the glass in winter too — when low December sun could instead be welcomed in for warmth. A point for the next (systems) phase.
